After all, the Sky Deer Goddess was from the Eight Wastelands and Ten Domains, a different realm from theirs. There would inevitably be a gap between them.

Despite his monotonous voice, many True Daoists’ countenances turned slightly unpleasant while their hearts skipped a beat as they sensed the unexplainable intimidation.

 

[He is as protective of his close ones as ever.]

He didn’t want to cross Gu Changge when the final battle was just ahead of them. Besides, the man had made it clear that the Sky Deer Goddess was his. Hence, only death would await him if he dared lay a finger on the woman. More importantly, there were rumours that the Gu Family’s Ancestor, who had annihilated Purple Mansion, had arrived. He just hadn’t shown himself, and it worried the supreme expert.

 

After all, the Great Elder of Purple Mansion was one of the best there was. In fact, some believed he would have long advanced into the Immortal Realm if heaven allowed it. However, the mighty Great Elder of Purple Mansion eventually died from one attack from the Gu Family Ancestor.

 

As a True Daoist, the supreme expert naturally took such incidents seriously, never daring to fool around with his own life.

 

Meanwhile, the Sky Deer Goddess looked somewhat stupefied at Gu Changge, for she, too, was surprised that Gu Changge would stand up for her at this time. An indescribably peculiar feeling arose within her.

 

[Well, it looks like he’s not entirely heartless after all, or is it just that the issue happened to have upset him? After all, that True Daoist from that Ancient Royal Family was also inadvertently provoking him.]

 

“Remember who you are now. You’re part of the Gu Family. They’re making nothing of the Gu Family if just about anybody can bully you,” said Gu Changge plainly as he gently held her hand.

 

The Sky Deer Goddess nodded as her eyes twinkled. Though she knew he was mostly saying it to the other True Daoists present, she still couldn’t help feeling touched. To the outside world, she was Gu Changge’s concubine, which in turn, made her a member of the Gu Family, and even though Gu Changge made nothing of this role, it still had to do with the Gu Family’s pride and dignity.

The Upper Realm Army breached the Aether Abyss and descended from above the sky. The phenomenon of soldiers unceasingly arriving was even more terrifying than the boundless mist, as it shook those from the Eight Wastelands and Ten Domains.

 

If one were to look down from the sky, one would find ancient battleships and soldiers within tens of millions of kilometres radius, consisting of the Ancient Clan that travelled with stars on their backs, with massive war beasts as large as mountains – monsters from the Godly Behemoth Clan.

 

These clans were born for war, innately aggressive beings with unimaginable power. Just a stomp of their feet would shake the sky and cause the earth to split, creating a rift as thick Chaos Qi burst out, engulfing the world.

 

After the massive mist arrived outside Sky Deer City, the grave-looking soldiers overlooked the place on the ancient battleships.
