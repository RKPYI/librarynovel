Gu Changge said nothing but took an inexplicable glance at Jia Nan. He naturally wouldn’t state his name at this time. After all, he had even concealed his face. So, this princess might not find out his true identity even if she investigated later.

 

After all, he should be recuperating in Black Tortoise Empire, unable to go anywhere else. Thus, he ignored this question entirely and stated his demand instead. “How many Eternal Overcast Sources from the Land of Eternal Overcast do you guys have at hand?”

“Eternal Overcast Sources?” Jia Nan was taken aback. She wasn’t really bothered that Gu Changge didn’t talk about himself, nor his background. However, she was honestly a little surprised and unsure why he would ask about the Eternal Overcast Sources.

By right, as a human cultivator, even as mysterious and powerful as Gu Changge could get, he should be avoiding something that could devour most matters like the Eternal Overcast Sources at all cost.

[Has he actually come to find the Eternal Overcast Sources in hopes of finding a way to crack it? After all, the Eternal Overcast Sources was something that not even the Eternal Overcast Kingdom would want anything to do with. Moreover, the birth of an Eternal Overcast Source was very mysterious. Even if they had some stored, there weren’t many of them.

Sometimes, they were compelled to place it in a certain area to create some crisis, because the Eternal Overcast Source would spread like the wind to a wildfire, unstoppable, once it started.

That said, the many Eternal Overcast Fog in the Upper Realm actually had nothing to do with the Eternal Overcast Kingdom. In fact, her arrival here was due to the sudden eruption of Yin Energy and expansion of the Eternal Overcast Fog.

“We actually have a shortage ourselves, but I can still give you some if you want. As for what will happen after you touch upon the Eternal Overcast Sources, I’m sure you know well. I don’t want to see you getting devoured by the Eternal Overcast Source before our exchange is complete,” Jia Nan emphasised, to which Gu Changge nodded plainly. “You don’t have to worry about that, Your Highness. I’m well aware of this.”

“That’s good.” Jia Nan nodded while pulling out a black token from within her sleeve. It looked like a chess piece, but Gu Changge could feel the spatial fluctuations contained within.

“This is a Space Piece. I will use this to inform you if anything comes up, and also, I’ll transport the Eternal Overcast Source you need through this thing,” Jia Nan disclosed as she handed the black chess piece, forged by unknown material, to Gu Changge, who scrutinised it after taking it. “Forged from the bones of a void beast.” He nodded. â€œNo wonder it has such properties.”

Thus, Jia Nan bid him farewell and left shortly after, heading back on the black boat with the others from the Kingdom, with no intention of descending onto the outside world.

The man in purple and gold armour, on the other hand, took a dreaded glance at Gu Changge from afar. The fear and apprehension he had experienced earlier still haunted him, and he couldn’t get over it for a long time.


He had no idea what his sister and Gu Changge said for her to choose to turn the boat around and head back to the Eternal Overcast Kingdom, but the trauma Gu Changge left him would surely follow him for some time.

He then chucked the Space Piece into storage and also sealed off the wisp of divine sense attached to it.

He knew that her divine sense was attached to the Space Piece, then again, it could also be a sliver of her Divine Soul, but either way, she could not possibly fool him.

As someone in the Sacred Emperor Realm, she had a plethora of means, but Gu Changge couldn’t be bothered to expose her as this sliver of her Divine Soul might actually come in handy in the future.


That said, given that this Divine Soul might detect his whereabouts and whatnot, Gu Changge sealed off this Space Piece and the connection it had to her, tantamount to trapping it all in the chess piece.

At this time, perhaps not even Jia Nan was aware that she had pretty much voluntarily handed her weakness over to Gu Changge.

As for the news Jia Nan would deliver to him, Gu Changge had no intentions of bothering about it to begin with. Once the Eternal Overcast Source was in his hands, he would have the final say in everything, no? Thereafter, in a flash, he travelled to the area he had sensed the Eternal Overcast Source earlier.

Over there, vast dark energy surged, extremely thick grey mist rolled, and incredibly ancient runes twinkled, looking like the purest source of the universe, which couldn’t be dated, formed by hundreds of millions of black light, which could devour everything. The Principles, Great Dao, and space near the area were constantly crumbling, reforming, and finally merging chaotically. Even a Sacred Emperor Realm expert could feel their hearts palpitating when they see such a sight..

It seems that as it spreads and devours, even space and Principles are being broken down. Guess not even runes made by a Sacred Emperor expert could hold off against this.]

Jia Nan never doubted Gu Changge’s Oath at that time. After all, it was the most serious oath a cultivator could make. No one would dare disregard it. That said, the man agreed to the exchange too easily. It raised a red flag within her, and she thought she needed to probe and keep an eye on him.
